## Design Notes

### Difficulty

Character count alone is a naive proxy for difficulty. A better signal is **total jamo count** across the word: a 3-character word with complex vowels and stacked consonants (받침) may have significantly more jamo than a 4-character word composed of simple CV syllables. When difficulty tiers are introduced, consider scoring words by total decomposed jamo count rather than syllable block count. This also interacts with the jamo pool given to the player — more jamo in the word means a larger pool, more distractors, and harder composition decisions.
